Moneytor

This is a UX/UI case study of a responsive web app. Moneytor is a money saving app that allows you to organize and optimize your spending and savings and to efficiently work towards your saving goals.

Why Moneytor?

For everyday people, a world of finance could be overwhelming with its numbers, graphs and jargon. People need a tool that will simplify their finance making it approachable regardless of their level of financial knowledge.

Skills employed

Incorporate design system to create a responsive website, web app, and/or mobile app.Also conducted user interviews in order to get real life opinions on the design and layout of this responsive app.

Tools: Adobe XD

User Testing and Feedback

After creating the high fidelity wireframes, I have reached out to 3 users to ask for responses and feedback. Here is the summary from these users feedback:

Positive Feedback

  • Clean and simple design.
  • Name fits the logo.
  • Dashboard screen is well designed.
  • Color palette is simple, making the app eye-catching.
  • Easy to navigate.

Constructive Feedback

  • Replace the continue option with maybe “ Next Button” on “Welcome screen”.
  • There is no “back or exit” option in “Find your bank account” screen.
  • On Screen “Connect your bank account” - Add text “ Bank Account” in the connect button, so the button will be more clear as “ Connect your bank account”.

Design Adjustments

Upon receiving these feedbacks, I went back to my project and made some modifications incorporating their suggestions and applying them to my design.

Replace the continue option with maybe “Next Button” on “Welcome screen”.

Add the “back or exit” option in “Find your bank account” screen.

Add text “ Bank Account” in the connect button, so the button will be more clear as “Connect your bank account”.
